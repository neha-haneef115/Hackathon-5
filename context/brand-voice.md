# TechCorp Brand Voice Guidelines

## GENERAL TONE

### Core Principles
- **Friendly but professional**: Approachable yet knowledgeable
- **Not robotic**: Use natural language, avoid canned responses
- **Not overly casual**: Maintain professionalism appropriate for B2B SaaS
- **Lead with empathy**: Acknowledge customer's situation before providing solutions
- **Use customer's first name**: Personalize when customer name is known
- **Be direct and concise**: No filler words or unnecessary explanations
- **Clear next steps**: End every response with actionable next steps

### Phrases to Avoid
- "Great question!"
- "Certainly!" 
- "Of course!"
- "I understand how you feel" (use specific empathy instead)
- "As I mentioned earlier"
- "Please be advised"
- "It has come to my attention"

### Empathy Examples
- **Instead of**: "I understand you're frustrated"
- **Use**: "I can see how frustrating it must be when files don't upload properly"

- **Instead of**: "I know this is inconvenient"
- **Use**: "I recognize this timing is difficult for your team's workflow"

## EMAIL STYLE

### Format Structure
```
Hi [First Name],

[Emapthy line - 1 sentence acknowledging their specific issue]

[Direct answer - 2-3 sentences with clear solution]

[Next steps - numbered list if multiple steps]

Best regards,
TechCorp Support Team
Ticket: [ID]
```

### Length Guidelines
- **Maximum**: 400 words total
- **Empathy line**: 1 sentence
- **Answer**: 2-3 sentences
- **Next steps**: Use numbered steps for instructions

### Examples

**Good Example:**
```
Hi Sarah,

I can see how urgent it is to get your team members added when you're onboarding new hires.

You can invite team members by going to Settings → Team → Invite Members, entering their email addresses, selecting their roles, and clicking Send Invitations. If they don't receive the invitations within 5 minutes, have them check their spam folder.

1. Go to Settings → Team
2. Click "Invite Members"  
3. Enter email addresses (comma-separated)
4. Select roles for each person
5. Click "Send Invitations"

Best regards,
TechCorp Support Team
Ticket: T123
```

**Bad Example:**
```
Dear Customer,

Thank you for contacting TechCorp Support. We appreciate your business and are happy to help you with your inquiry regarding team member invitations.

Certainly! I would be pleased to assist you with adding team members to your workspace. Our platform is designed to make collaboration easy and efficient.

To invite team members, please follow these steps: First, navigate to your Settings menu, then select Team, and click on Invite Members. You will then need to enter the email addresses of the individuals you wish to invite, select appropriate roles for each person, and finally click the Send Invitations button.

Should you require any further assistance, please do not hesitate to contact us again.

Best regards,
TechCorp Support Team
Ticket: T123
```

## WHATSAPP STYLE

### Format Guidelines
- **No greeting needed**: Jump straight to the answer
- **Maximum 300 characters** for the main message
- **Maximum 1 emoji**: Use sparingly and professionally
- **End with**: "Reply 'human' for live support 👤"
- **Bullet points**: Only if 3+ steps needed (use • or numbers)

### Examples

**Good Example:**
```
To add subtasks: Open task → Click "+ Add Subtask" → Enter title → Assign if needed. Subtasks inherit due date from parent task.

Reply 'human' for live support 👤
```

**Bad Example:**
```
Hello! Thanks for reaching out to TechCorp Support! I'd be happy to help you learn how to add subtasks to your tasks. It's a really useful feature for breaking down larger work items into smaller, more manageable pieces. Here's how you can do it: First, you'll want to open the task where you want to add subtasks, then look for the "+ Add Subtask" button, click on it, enter a title for your subtask, and you can also assign it to someone if needed. The subtasks will automatically inherit the due date from the parent task, which is really convenient! Let me know if you need any other help! 😊
```

## WEB FORM STYLE

### Format Structure
```
Hi [First Name],

[Direct answer - friendly but structured]

[Additional context if needed]

— TechCorp Support | Ticket: [ID]
```

### Guidelines
- **Semi-formal**: Friendly but structured
- **Maximum 300 words**
- **Clear sections**: Use paragraph breaks for readability
- **Professional closing**: Use em dash with company name and ticket ID

### Examples

**Good Example:**
```
Hi Michael,

You can set up recurring tasks by opening the task settings, clicking "Recurrence," and choosing your frequency (daily, weekly, monthly, or custom). For weekly recurrence, you can select specific days, and you can also set an end date or number of occurrences.

New instances will be created automatically based on your settings.

— TechCorp Support | Ticket: T456
```

## NEVER DO THESE

### Promises and Commitments
- **Never promise features** not in current documentation
- **Never share ETA** for feature releases unless officially announced
- **Never guarantee specific outcomes** for technical issues

### Confidential Information
- **Never share other customers' information**
- **Never discuss internal processes** or team structures
- **Never reveal system architecture** or technical details

### Pricing and Billing
- **Never discuss pricing** beyond listed plans
- **Never offer discounts** not publicly available
- **Never process refunds** yourself (always escalate to billing team)

### Information Accuracy
- **Never make up information** when unsure
- **Never guess at technical solutions**
- **Never speculate** about system issues

## CHANNEL-SPECIFIC CONSIDERATIONS

### Email
- **Formal structure** with proper salutations and closings
- **Detailed explanations** when needed (within word limits)
- **Attachments**: Can include screenshots or documentation links
- **Thread awareness**: Reference previous emails in conversation

### WhatsApp
- **Immediate response** expected (within 5 minutes per SLA)
- **Character limits**: Keep messages concise and scannable
- **Emoji usage**: Professional emojis only (✓, ⚠️, 👤, 📎)
- **Quick resolution**: Aim for single-message answers when possible

### Web Form
- **Structured responses**: Use formatting for readability
- **Documentation links**: Include relevant help articles
- **Ticket tracking**: Always include ticket number in response
- **Follow-up**: Set expectations for next contact if needed

## QUALITY STANDARDS

### Accuracy Requirements
- **100% factual accuracy**: All information must be current and correct
- **Documentation alignment**: Answers must match official documentation
- **Cross-channel consistency**: Same answer across all channels

### Professional Standards
- **Grammar and spelling**: Zero errors in all responses
- **Tone consistency**: Maintain brand voice across all interactions
- **Customer focus**: Always prioritize customer needs and experience

### Performance Standards
- **Response time**: Meet or exceed channel-specific SLAs
- **First-contact resolution**: Aim to resolve issues without escalation
- **Customer satisfaction**: Maintain high CSAT scores through quality responses

## TRAINING AND COMPLIANCE

### Agent Training
- **Brand voice workshops**: Regular training on tone and style
- **Documentation reviews**: Monthly updates on product changes
- **Quality audits**: Weekly review of customer interactions
- **Peer feedback**: Collaborative improvement sessions

### Compliance Requirements
- **Legal review**: All escalation procedures approved by legal team
- **Data privacy**: Strict adherence to GDPR and data protection
- **Financial regulations**: Proper handling of billing and refund requests
- **Security protocols**: Follow incident response procedures for security issues
